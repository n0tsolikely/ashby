ASHBY CODEX — 6.4
SCOPES AS THE WORLD MODEL

Scopes are the world model of Ashby.

They are not labels.
They are not folders.
They are not convenience groupings.

Scopes are the formal structure through which Ashby understands,
reasons about, and acts within reality.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT A SCOPE IS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A scope is a bounded context that defines:
- what exists
- what may be acted upon
- what state is authoritative
- what authority applies
- what history is relevant

Every action Ashby takes occurs inside exactly one scope.

There is no global, unscoped action.
There is no unscoped state.
There is no unscoped memory.

If something cannot be placed in a scope,
it does not exist to Ashby.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY SCOPES ARE REQUIRED
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Real-world systems are not flat.

They are:
- hierarchical
- overlapping
- partially isolated
- governed by boundaries

Without scopes, systems:
- infer context implicitly
- leak state between domains
- apply actions too broadly
- lose authority boundaries
- become unsafe to automate

Ashby treats scoping as mandatory
to prevent these failures.

Scopes make context explicit.
They make authority visible.
They make automation safe.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SCOPE OWNERSHIP AND AUTHORITY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each scope defines:
- what Ashby is allowed to control
- what Ashby may observe
- what Ashby may remember
- what Ashby may automate

Authority is scoped.

An action that is valid in one scope
may be forbidden in another.

State recorded in one scope
does not silently affect another.

This prevents:
- cross-domain contamination
- accidental overreach
- unsafe automation
- ambiguous responsibility

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SCOPES AND STATE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HouseState is not global.

State is always scoped.

Each scope maintains:
- its own beliefs
- its own confidence levels
- its own history
- its own uncertainty

State may be summarized upward.
State may never leak sideways.

A higher-level scope may:
- observe aggregate state
- reason about child scopes
- issue policies affecting children

A lower-level scope may never:
- override parent authority
- assume parent state
- act outside its bounds

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SCOPES AND EXECUTION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Execution targets are resolved within scopes.

When Ashby executes an action:
- the target must exist in the scope
- the adapter must support the scope
- verification occurs within the scope
- results are recorded in the scope

An adapter that cannot respect scope boundaries
is incompatible with Ashby.

This ensures that execution respects:
- physical boundaries
- organizational boundaries
- permission boundaries

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SCOPES AND MEMORY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Memory is scoped.

Ashby remembers actions,
outcomes,
and uncertainty
within the scope where they occurred.

Memory does not bleed across scopes.

This prevents:
- false continuity
- incorrect inference
- unsafe assumptions

When Ashby recalls history,
it recalls it relative to scope.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SCOPES AS THE FOUNDATION FOR GROWTH
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Scopes are what allow Ashby to grow safely.

New domains are added by:
- defining new scopes
- attaching modules to scopes
- assigning authority explicitly

No new domain requires redefining the world model.

It requires defining a new scope.

This allows Ashby to:
- expand without rewriting law
- add automation without increasing risk
- support multiple environments simultaneously

Scopes are the mechanism
by which Ashby understands the world
without flattening it.

If scopes are weakened,
Ashby becomes unsafe.

If scopes are enforced,
Ashby remains coherent.

Scopes are not an implementation detail.

They are Ashby’s model of reality.

END OF 6.4
