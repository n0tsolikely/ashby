ASHBY CODEX — 5.13
COMMANDMENT XII — ASHBY IS A PLATFORM FIRST

Ashby is a platform first.

This commandment exists to prevent Ashby from being defined
by any single use case, domain, or moment in time.

Ashby is not a product that later became extensible.
Ashby is an extensible system by design.

Every decision in Ashby must be evaluated through this lens:
“Does this strengthen the platform, or does it privilege one product?”

If a choice benefits one product
but harms the platform’s integrity,
that choice is rejected.

This commandment exists because product-first systems
inevitably fracture.

When a system is defined by a single product:
- features become entangled
- edge cases harden into law
- temporary hacks become permanent
- future domains become harder to integrate
- refactors become existential risks

Ashby avoids this by enforcing platform primacy.

Products in Ashby are expressions.
They are configurations.
They are profiles composed of:
- enabled modules
- enabled agents
- execution paths
- feature flags
- entitlements

No product owns the engine.
No product owns truth.
No product owns state.
No product owns scope.

The platform owns these things.

This commandment allows Ashby to support:
- homes and properties
- businesses and operations
- creative workflows
- monitoring systems
- future domains not yet imagined

without redefining itself.

Platform-first also means restraint.

Ashby does not add core features
simply because a product needs them.
Core features are added only when
multiple future products would require them
and the addition preserves architectural law.

This commandment protects Ashby from becoming
a collection of tightly coupled solutions.

Ashby remains coherent
because the platform comes first.

Any design that treats Ashby
as “the smart home version,”
“the meetings version,”
or “the inventory version”
violates this commandment.

Ashby is the platform.
Products are temporary.

This hierarchy must never be reversed.

END OF 5.13
