ASHBY CODEX — 7.5
SCOPE HIERARCHIES AND CONTAINMENT

Scopes are hierarchical.

They form containment relationships
that define authority, inheritance, and limits.

Hierarchy is not optional.
It is required to model reality safely.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY HIERARCHY EXISTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Real environments are nested.

A room exists inside a zone.
A zone exists inside a property.
A property exists inside an organization.

Without hierarchy:
- authority becomes ambiguous
- automation becomes unsafe
- overrides become unpredictable
- explanations become unclear

Hierarchy allows Ashby to reason
about where actions apply
and where they must stop.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PARENT AND CHILD SCOPES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A parent scope:
- defines upper bounds of authority
- may impose policy on child scopes
- may observe aggregated state
- may restrict child behavior

A child scope:
- operates within parent constraints
- owns its local state
- may not override parent authority
- may not assume parent intent

Authority flows downward.
Constraints flow downward.
Overrides never flow upward implicitly.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTAINMENT RULES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Containment is explicit.

A scope must declare:
- its parent scope
- its containment boundaries
- its authority limits

Implicit containment is forbidden.

If containment cannot be expressed clearly,
the scope definition is invalid.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STATE AND CONTAINMENT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

State is contained.

Child scope state:
- does not silently modify parent state
- may be summarized upward explicitly
- retains its own confidence and history

Parent scopes may reason
about child scope state,
but must not overwrite it.

This prevents:
- hidden coupling
- false global assumptions
- cascading errors

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTAINMENT AND AUTOMATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Automation respects containment.

An automation defined at a parent scope:
- applies only to contained scopes
- may be restricted by child configuration
- must respect local uncertainty

An automation defined at a child scope:
- must not escape its boundary
- must not affect siblings
- must not bypass parent policy

Containment defines blast radius.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSOLUTE CONSTRAINTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Scopes must never:
- infer containment
- collapse hierarchy for convenience
- bypass parent constraints
- override child state silently

Any design that flattens scope hierarchy
violates Ashby’s world model.

Hierarchy is how Ashby
models complexity without losing control.

END OF 7.5
