ASHBY CODEX — 10.6
ACTIONRESULT AS REALITY

ActionResult is the only representation of reality
that Ashby is allowed to trust.

It is not a convenience object.
It is not a logging artifact.
It is the authoritative evidence boundary
between execution and belief.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT AN ACTIONRESULT IS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

An ActionResult represents the observable outcome
of a single attempted action.

It records:
- what was attempted
- how execution was invoked
- what was observed
- what failed or succeeded
- what uncertainty remains
- timing and correlation data

ActionResult does not interpret meaning.
It records evidence.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY ACTIONRESULT IS AUTHORITATIVE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Without ActionResult,
systems infer success.

Inference creates lies.

ActionResult forces Ashby to confront reality
as it occurred,
not as it was intended.

No action may influence state,
truth,
or language
without an ActionResult.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACTIONRESULT AND CONFIDENCE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ActionResult feeds the Truth Gate.

From ActionResult,
the engine derives:
- VERIFIED outcomes
- LAST_KNOWN outcomes
- FAILED outcomes
- UNKNOWN outcomes

Confidence is not reported by adapters.
Confidence is assigned by the engine.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACTIONRESULT AND PARTIAL SUCCESS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Partial success is explicit.

An ActionResult may represent:
- some targets succeeded
- some targets failed
- some targets are uncertain

Partial success must never
be collapsed into success.

Ambiguity is preserved.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACTIONRESULT AND MEMORY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ActionResults are recorded as memory.

They form the factual history
that explanations and audits rely on.

Memory without ActionResult
is fiction.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACTIONRESULT AND RETRIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Retries produce additional ActionResults.

Each attempt is recorded.

A later success does not erase
earlier failure.

This prevents retroactive rewriting of history.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSOLUTE CONSTRAINTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

No component may:
- invent ActionResults
- bypass ActionResult creation
- merge ActionResults implicitly
- discard ActionResults silently

ActionResult is the spine of execution truth.

If ActionResult is compromised,
Ashby cannot be trusted.

END OF 10.6
