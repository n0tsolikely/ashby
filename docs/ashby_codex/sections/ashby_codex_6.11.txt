ASHBY CODEX — 6.11
CONTROL SYNC GOVERNANCE

Control Sync is the governance mechanism of Ashby.

It exists to ensure that foundational change
is deliberate, visible, and accountable.

Without Control Sync,
Ashby would drift invisibly
until its identity was lost.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT CONTROL SYNC IS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A Control Sync is a formal decision process
required for any change that affects Ashby’s core law.

It is not a meeting for convenience.
It is not a procedural ritual.
It is a safeguard.

Control Sync exists to pause momentum
long enough for judgment to occur.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHEN CONTROL SYNC IS REQUIRED
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A Control Sync is required when a change affects:

- engine responsibilities
- truth semantics
- state authority
- scope model
- WHAT vs HOW separation
- removability guarantees
- governance assumptions
- rebuildability guarantees

These changes redefine what Ashby is.

They are not incremental improvements.
They are identity changes.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHEN CONTROL SYNC IS NOT REQUIRED
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Control Sync is not required for:

- adding or removing modules
- adding or removing agents
- adding or removing adapters
- adding vendors or protocols
- creating or modifying profiles
- tuning policies within existing law
- fixing bugs that do not alter semantics

These are expected forms of growth
that occur within Ashby’s defined boundaries.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE CONTROL SYNC PROCESS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A Control Sync requires:

1) Explicit articulation of the proposed change
2) Clear identification of affected laws or principles
3) Explanation of why existing law is insufficient
4) Description of the new law or modification
5) Acknowledgment of consequences
6) Update to the Codex reflecting the change

No Control Sync is valid
without Codex update.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY CONTROL SYNC IS NON-NEGOTIABLE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Systems do not fail from large changes.
They fail from small, unexamined ones.

Control Sync prevents:
- accidental redefinition of the engine
- erosion of truth enforcement
- silent scope expansion
- normalization of exceptions
- loss of rebuildability

It protects future builders
from inheriting invisible decisions.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTROL SYNC AND AUTHORITY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Control Sync does not centralize power.
It centralizes responsibility.

Any participant in a Control Sync
must accept that:

- the decision becomes permanent law
- future work is constrained by it
- the Codex reflects it explicitly

There is no informal override.

If a change is uncomfortable,
that discomfort is a signal.

If a change feels “too big to explain,”
it is too big to implement without Control Sync.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSOLUTE CONSEQUENCES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Any foundational change made without Control Sync
is considered invalid.

If discovered,
the system must be realigned
to the Codex immediately.

Control Sync exists
so that Ashby remains intentional
even as it evolves.

Governance is not overhead.

It is how Ashby survives time.

END OF 6.11
