ASHBY CODEX — 8.3
COGNITION SUBSYSTEMS

Cognition subsystems define how Ashby thinks.

They do not define what Ashby is.
They define how reasoning is structured,
sequenced,
and constrained.

Cognition exists to transform intent into action
without violating truth, state, or scope law.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE ROLE OF COGNITION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cognition is the internal decision machinery of the engine.

It is responsible for:
- interpreting normalized intent
- resolving scope and context
- selecting policy paths
- deciding whether to act, clarify, or refuse
- sequencing reasoning steps
- preparing ActionRequests

Cognition never executes actions.
It prepares decisions for execution layers.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUBSYSTEM, NOT MONOLITH
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cognition is not a single algorithm.

It is composed of subsystems,
each with a bounded responsibility.

This prevents:
- god-reasoners
- hidden coupling
- untraceable decisions
- brittle logic growth

Each subsystem must be:
- explicit
- testable
- replaceable
- auditable

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CORE COGNITION SUBSYSTEMS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cognition subsystems include, but are not limited to:

- Intent normalization
- Scope resolution
- Ambiguity detection
- Policy evaluation
- Session management
- Safety checks
- Action planning
- Clarification generation

These are representative categories,
not a fixed inventory.

The shape is invariant.
The instances may evolve.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SEQUENCING AND FLOW
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cognition is sequential and gated.

Each stage:
- consumes explicit inputs
- produces explicit outputs
- may halt the pipeline
- may require clarification
- may refuse to proceed

No stage may:
- assume success of a later stage
- skip required checks
- bypass scope or truth law

This sequencing makes decisions traceable.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COGNITION AND EXPLAINABILITY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cognition must leave a trail.

Every decision must be explainable
in terms of:
- which subsystem acted
- what inputs were considered
- why a path was chosen
- why alternatives were rejected

Opaque reasoning is forbidden.

If a decision cannot be explained,
the cognition design is invalid.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COGNITION AND GROWTH
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

As Ashby grows,
cognition complexity increases.

Subsystem boundaries prevent collapse.

New reasoning capabilities must:
- fit within existing subsystems
- or introduce a new explicit subsystem
- without modifying unrelated logic

Ad-hoc reasoning is forbidden.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSOLUTE CONSTRAINTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cognition subsystems must never:
- execute actions
- define truth
- own state
- bypass scope checks
- invent certainty

Cognition prepares decisions.
The engine governs truth.
Execution proves reality.

This separation preserves honesty
even as reasoning becomes more powerful.

END OF 8.3
