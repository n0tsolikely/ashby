ASHBY CODEX — 8.12
SUPPORT, OBSERVABILITY, AND LONGEVITY

Support and observability exist to ensure Ashby remains
understandable, debuggable, and trustworthy over time.

Longevity is not accidental.
It is engineered.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUPPORT AS STRUCTURAL CLARITY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Support in Ashby is not reactive firefighting.

It is enabled by:
- explicit state
- recorded history
- traceable decisions
- scoped authority
- explainable failures

A system that cannot explain itself
cannot be supported safely.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBSERVABILITY AS FIRST-CLASS REQUIREMENT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Observability is mandatory.

Ashby must expose:
- what actions were attempted
- what execution paths were used
- what succeeded or failed
- what evidence exists
- what is currently uncertain
- what confidence has decayed

Logs are not enough.
Signals must be meaningful.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBSERVABILITY WITHOUT LYING
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Observability must reflect truth.

It must never:
- smooth failure
- collapse uncertainty
- infer success
- reconstruct narrative

Metrics that hide reality
are worse than no metrics.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LONGEVITY THROUGH DISCIPLINE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Longevity emerges from discipline.

Ashby survives time by:
- resisting convenience
- enforcing structure
- preventing silent drift
- governing change explicitly
- preserving rebuildability

Support tooling may change.
Observability tooling may evolve.
The underlying guarantees must not.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INCIDENTS AND POST-MORTEMS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When Ashby fails,
failure must be analyzable.

Post-mortems must be able to answer:
- what happened
- where it happened
- why it happened
- what evidence exists
- what assumptions failed

If these questions cannot be answered,
the architecture is insufficient.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EVOLUTION WITHOUT EROSION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

As Ashby evolves:
- new tooling may be added
- new diagnostics may appear
- new interfaces may exist

None may:
- bypass truth enforcement
- obscure state authority
- weaken scope law
- hide uncertainty

Longevity is preserved
by refusing erosion.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSOLUTE CONSTRAINTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Support and observability must never:
- define behavior
- invent certainty
- bypass Action-Gated Truth
- replace explanation with metrics

They exist to reveal Ashby,
not to reshape it.

This is how Ashby remains operable
years after its creation.

END OF 8.12
