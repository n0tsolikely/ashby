ASHBY CODEX — 7.3
ZONES AS A SPECIALIZATION OF SCOPE

Zones are a specialized form of scope.

They exist to bridge human language and physical reality
without compromising Ashby’s world model.

A zone is not merely a room name.
A zone is a controllable physical region
with defined boundaries, authority, and state.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY ZONES EXIST
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Humans think and speak in zones.

They say:
- “the bedroom”
- “upstairs”
- “the backyard”
- “the shop”
- “the east side of the property”

These phrases describe regions,
not individual devices.

Zones allow Ashby to map this natural language
onto explicit, enforceable scopes.

Without zones:
- language becomes ambiguous
- automation becomes unsafe
- actions become overly broad
- confirmation logic becomes inconsistent

Zones resolve this tension.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ZONES VS ROOMS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rooms are human labels.
Zones are operational scopes.

A single room label may:
- map to one zone
- map to part of a zone
- map to multiple zones

A zone may:
- contain multiple rooms
- contain part of a room
- exist without a room label

This separation prevents
human naming from defining reality.

Reality is defined by zones.
Language adapts to zones,
not the reverse.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ZONE AUTHORITY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Zones define authority boundaries for physical action.

Within a zone, Ashby may:
- control devices assigned to the zone
- aggregate sensor data
- apply automation policies
- reason about state cohesively

Across zones, Ashby must:
- respect boundaries
- require explicit intent
- avoid implicit spillover

A command such as:
“turn off the lights”

is incomplete without zone resolution.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ZONE COMPOSITION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Zones are composable.

A zone may:
- exist inside another zone
- overlap with another zone
- share devices with another zone (explicitly)

Composition must always be explicit.

Implicit overlap is forbidden.

This allows:
- hierarchical control
- partial automation
- targeted overrides
without ambiguity.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ZONES AND STATE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Zone state is authoritative for that region.

Ashby tracks:
- device state by zone
- confidence by zone
- history by zone
- uncertainty by zone

Zone-level state allows Ashby to:
- explain what changed where
- isolate failures
- degrade confidence locally
- avoid global assumptions

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ZONES AND AUTOMATION SAFETY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Zones are a primary safety mechanism.

Automation applies to zones,
not to the entire system.

This allows:
- scoped automation
- bounded blast radius
- predictable behavior

If zone resolution is ambiguous,
Ashby must pause or clarify.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSOLUTE CONSTRAINTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Zones must never:
- be inferred silently
- be overridden by convenience
- collapse into global state

Any design that allows actions
to bypass zone boundaries
violates Ashby’s world model.

Zones are how Ashby understands
and safely controls physical space.

END OF 7.3
