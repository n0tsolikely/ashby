ASHBY CODEX — 8.9
PACKAGING, PROFILES, AND PRODUCTS

Packaging defines how Ashby is delivered
without redefining what Ashby is.

Profiles define behavior.
Packaging defines distribution.
Products are composed expressions of both.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PACKAGING AS DISTRIBUTION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Packaging determines:
- how Ashby is deployed
- how components are bundled
- how updates are delivered
- how environments are targeted

Packaging may vary by:
- hardware platform
- customer type
- deployment model
- regulatory environment

Packaging must never:
- alter engine law
- redefine truth semantics
- change scope behavior
- introduce hidden coupling

Packaging is operational,
not architectural.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROFILES AS BEHAVIOR SELECTION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Profiles select behavior.

A profile determines:
- which modules are enabled
- which agents are active
- which execution paths are allowed
- which automations are permitted
- which safety constraints apply

Profiles do not:
- fork code
- redefine the engine
- modify truth enforcement
- bypass governance

Profiles are configuration,
not divergence.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PRODUCTS AS COMPOSITION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Products are composed from:
- the engine
- selected modules
- selected agents
- profiles
- packaging

A product is not a fork.
It is a constrained expression
of the same platform.

Examples include:
- a residential automation product
- an enterprise operations product
- a creator workflow product

These are representative,
not exhaustive.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY THIS SEPARATION MATTERS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Without separation:
- product needs pollute the engine
- packaging hacks become law
- profiles become exceptions
- rebuildability is lost

Ashby enforces this separation
to remain a platform.

A product may be discontinued.
The platform must not be altered.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
UPDATES AND EVOLUTION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Packaging may change frequently.
Profiles may evolve.
Products may come and go.

The engine must remain stable.

If a change requires modifying the engine
to support a product,
that change must be evaluated via Control Sync.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSOLUTE CONSTRAINTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Packaging must never:
- embed domain logic
- bypass removability
- hide execution assumptions

Profiles must never:
- weaken safety constraints
- redefine scope law
- introduce special cases

Products must never:
- redefine the platform
- hardcode behavior into the engine

This separation allows Ashby
to ship broadly
without losing itself.

END OF 8.9
